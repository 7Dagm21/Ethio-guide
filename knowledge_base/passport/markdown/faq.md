# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) — Ethiopian Passport Services

### 1. Can I renew my passport after it expires?
**Yes.** You can renew your passport after it has officially expired. There are no late renewal penalty fees, but you will not be able to book international travel or clear customs until your new passport is processed, printed, and collected.

### 2. Can I travel with an expired passport?
**No.** Airline boarding rules and international immigration laws strictly require a valid passport with at least **six months of remaining validity** before your travel date. If your passport has expired, you must apply for a renewal or obtain an Emergency Passport (Laissez-Passer) for immediate, one-way travel back to Ethiopia.

### 3. How long does passport processing take?
Processing times depend entirely on the service tier you select and pay for:
* **Standard Service:** 5 to 10 business days from your biometric appointment.
* **Urgent Service:** 5 business days.
* **Fast-Track Service:** 2 business days.
* **Diaspora Service:** 2 to 4 weeks if applying from an overseas Ethiopian embassy or consulate.

### 4. Can someone else collect my passport on my behalf?
**Only under strict legal exceptions.** Normally, you must collect your passport in person to verify your identity. However, someone else may collect it on your behalf only if they present:
* An officially authenticated **Power of Attorney (Wukilna)**.
* Your original physical application/payment receipt.
* Their own valid National ID (Fayda) or passport.

> **Note:** Parents or legal guardians may collect passports for their minor children by bringing the child's original birth certificate and the parent/guardian's valid ID.

### 5. Can I apply for an Ethiopian passport while living abroad?
**Yes.** If you are living outside of Ethiopia, you can apply online via the **Digital Invea** platform (accessible via web browser or mobile app). Once you submit your details and pay, you will schedule an appointment to complete your biometrics and collect the physical passport at the nearest designated Ethiopian Embassy or Consulate.

### 6. Can I change my biometric appointment date?
**Yes, but only under specific limits.** You can log back into your portal account before your scheduled date to reschedule your biometrics appointment to a different available slot. However, constantly rescheduling may push your processing timeline back, and changes are **not permitted** once your original appointment slot has already passed.

### 7. Can I cancel my application and get a refund?
**You can stop the process, but you cannot get a refund.** While you can choose not to attend your biometrics appointment (effectively canceling your application), all fees paid to the Immigration and Citizenship Service (ICS) are **strictly non-refundable**.

### 8. How long is the Ethiopian passport valid for?
* **Ages 25 and above:** Valid for 10 years.
* **Ages under 25 (including minors):** Valid for 5 years.

### 9. What should I do if my passport is physically damaged?
You must report it to the ICS as a **"Damaged Passport"** application rather than a standard renewal. You will have to surrender the physically damaged booklet at your appointment for verification. The standard replacement fee for a damaged passport is **13,000 ETB**.

### 10. What if I lose my application or payment receipt?
Your digital payment and application receipts are saved permanently under your account on the ePass/Digital Invea portal. Simply log in to your account, navigate to your active application, and download or print a fresh copy of your confirmation page and payment code.

### 11. Can I upload my required documents later?
**No.** The online application portal will not let you submit, pay for, or book an appointment for your application until all mandatory documents (such as your Fayda ID, birth certificate, and biometric photo) have been fully uploaded to the system. All uploads must be successfully completed during the initial online filing phase.
