# Ethiopian Passport Acquisition Procedures

## I. New Passport
Obtaining a new passport involves a structured process that transitions from a digital application to an in-person biometrics appointment.

### 1. Create Account
* **Action:** Visit the official registration portal at [ethiopianpassportservice.gov.et](https://www.ethiopianpassportservice.gov.et) (or use the **Digital Invea** mobile app if applying from the diaspora).
* **Details:** Sign up by registering with a valid email address and a local phone number. You will receive a verification code (OTP) via email or SMS to activate your account profile.

### 2. Fill Application
* **Action:** Log in, select **"New Passport Application"**, and fill out the personal information fields.
* **Details:** You must enter your details (Full Name, Date of Birth, Gender, Occupation, and Place of Birth) exactly as they appear on your legal identity papers. You will also need to input your National ID (Fayda) details.

### 3. Upload Documents
* **Action:** Scan and upload clean, clear JPEG/JPG files of your primary identifying documents.
* **Requirements:**
    * **National Digital ID (Fayda):** This is now a strict prerequisite for the application process.
    * **Authenticated Birth Certificate:** Required for all applicants under 40 years of age.
    * **Renewed Kebele ID / Local ID:** Showing your current residency status.
    * **Biometric Photo:** Must be 30 x 40 mm (minimum 600 DPI resolution) with a solid white background. Head straight, eyes open, neutral expression, and both ears fully visible. No glasses or headwear are permitted unless for documented medical or religious reasons.

### 4. Pay
* **Action:** Submit the application to generate your unique reference number and initiate payment.
* **Details:** Payments must be made electronically through integrated channels such as Telebirr, CBE Birr, or other supported online banking options.
    * **Standard Fee:** 5,000 ETB
    * **5-Day Urgent Service:** 20,000 ETB
    * **2-Day Fast-Track Service:** 25,000 ETB
    * *Note: For expedited options, you must upload valid proof of immediate travel, such as medical referral letters, university scholarship admissions, or urgent business invites.*

### 5. Appointment
* **Action:** Select your preferred ICS branch location (e.g., Main Office in Addis Ababa or regional offices) and book a calendar slot.
* **Details:** Print the booking confirmation receipt containing your appointment date, time, and office location.

### 6. Biometrics
* **Action:** Attend your in-person appointment at the scheduled time.
* **Details:** Bring the printed online payment receipt, appointment page, and the physical originals of all uploaded documents (Fayda Card, Kebele ID, Birth Certificate). ICS officers will physically verify your files, capture your fingerprints, and take your digital passport photo.

### 7. Passport Printing
* **Action:** Once biometrics are processed, your application moves to the printing stage.
* **Details:** Regular passports are printed within 5 to 10 business days from the biometrics date. Urgent options are printed within 2 or 5 business days depending on the fee paid.

### 8. Collection
* **Action:** You will receive an SMS notification stating that your passport is ready for pickup.
* **Details:** Bring your application confirmation slip and a valid ID to the designated collection counter at your chosen ICS branch to claim your physical passport.

## II. Renewal
If you already possess an Ethiopian passport and it has expired, is filling up with stamps, or requires personal detail updates, you can apply for a renewal.

### Requirements & Documents
* Your existing passport (Original and a copy of the biometric page).
* Your National Digital ID (Fayda).
* A renewed Kebele ID.
* A standard biometric photo.

### Fees
* **Standard Renewal:** 5,000 ETB
* **Renewal with Name/Photo Corrections:** 12,500 ETB
* **Expedited 5-Day Renewal:** 13,000 ETB base fee + 28,000 ETB express charge
* **Expedited 2-Day Renewal:** 13,000 ETB base fee + 33,000 ETB express charge

## III. Lost Passport
Replacing a lost passport requires additional security verification steps to mitigate identity theft and unauthorized document use.

* **What to Do:** File an online request for a lost passport replacement, then schedule your physical appointment.
* **Police Report Requirements:** You must visit your local police station, report the loss, and obtain a formal, stamped Police Report specifying that your passport was lost or stolen. You will be required to upload and bring this original document.
* **Additional Documents:**
    * Your Fayda ID.
    * Your renewed Kebele ID.
    * A physical photocopy of the lost passport (if available/highly recommended).
* **Fees:** Standard replacement for a lost passport costs 13,000 ETB.

---

## IV. Damaged Passport
If your passport is physically compromised (e.g., torn pages, severe water damage, or illegible bio-data), it is considered invalid for travel and must be replaced.

* **What to Do:** Present the physically damaged passport booklet to ICS officers for evaluation and deactivation.
* **Documents & Requirements:**
    * The original damaged passport booklet.
    * Your Fayda ID.
    * Renewed Kebele ID.
    * Standard passport photo upload.
* **Fees:** Standard replacement for a damaged passport is 13,000 ETB. For an expedited 2-day replacement, the fee is 33,000 ETB.

---

## V. Child Passport (Minors Under 18)
Passport applications for children under the age of 18 require parental or legal guardian authorization to prevent cross-border child trafficking and custody violations.

* **Birth Certificate:** A certified, authenticated Birth Certificate from the Vital Events Registration Agency (VERA) is mandatory.
* **Parent Requirements:**
    * A signed written Parental Consent Form from the mother, father, or legal guardian.
    * Original and clear photocopies of the parents' valid passports or renewed Kebele IDs.
* **Guardian Information:** If a legal guardian is handling the application instead of the parents, they must upload and bring the official, certified Court Guardianship Award Documents.
* **Biometrics Exception:** Children under the age of 14 are exempt from having their fingerprints scanned, but they must still accompany their parents to the ICS office to have their digital portrait photo captured.
